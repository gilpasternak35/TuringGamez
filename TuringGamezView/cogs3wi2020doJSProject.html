George Boole was a crucial figure in the history of computing. He was the inventor of Boolean Logic, which is the logic computers use for logic gates and calculations. He essentially made the claim that logic should be mathematic, rather than based on philosophy, and developed a whole branch of logic based on the values true or false. Additionally, he contributed several monumental papers about linear transformations and algebra.
